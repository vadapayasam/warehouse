Pain Points from Forums

What developers actually complain about. Not what marketplaces say they need.

1

Assets Ship Without Prefabs

Animated characters from Asset Store arrive without prefabs or documentation. Beginners get stuck on basic setup.

"Asset store animated character has no prefab, is there a simple tutorial as to how to fix?"
Unity Discussions, Getting Started
2

Losing Play Mode Work

Developers repeatedly lose changes made in Unity's play mode. Exiting play mode wipes unsaved work without warning.

"I built a toolkit called DevFlow after losing play mode changes one too many times. Five small editor tools that stopped me from throwing my keyboard."
Unity Community Showcases, 1,200+ upvotes
3

Grey Folder Navigation Hell

Large Unity projects become impossible to navigate with identical grey folders everywhere. Developers waste time hunting for files.

"Working in large Unity projects means identical grey folders everywhere. PieDesk 2 makes your project browser visual and fast."
Unity Community Showcases
4

Physics Netcode Complexity

Multiplayer physics games have no good networking solutions. DOTS vs GameObjects decision is painful.

"Multiplayer movement FPS — fast, physics-driven locomotion. Latency and jitter are major issues. DOTS vs GameObjects decision is difficult."
Unity News & General Discussion
5

Niche Genre Assets Missing

Developers with $200–300 budgets cannot find affordable, high-quality assets for specific genres like horror or visual novels.

"Music composer looking to join indie horror or experimental games."
itch.io Forums, Help Wanted (13,708 topics)

03

Where to Sell

Platform analysis for game asset distribution.

Unity Asset Store

70/30 split
  • Massive existing traffic
  • Built-in discovery
  • 2–3 day approval
  • Quality standards enforced
Best for: Full assets with polish

itch.io

90/10 split
  • Instant publishing
  • Indie-friendly audience
  • No approval process
  • Lower organic traffic
Best for: Rapid iteration, MVPs

Gumroad

~95% after fees
  • Direct fan relationships
  • Email marketing built-in
  • Pay-what-you-want
  • Build your own traffic
Best for: Bundles and packs

Humble Bundle

Bundle revenue
  • Massive volume potential
  • Curated inclusion only
  • Requires existing products
  • Lower per-unit revenue
Best for: Established catalogs

Action Plan

Step 1

Pick One Niche

Start with colored folders (proven demand), UI animations (high volume), or horror VFX (defensible niche). Do not build a generalist store.

Step 2

LLM Production Workflow

Use Claude/GPT for documentation, image generation for previews, GPT-4 for script templates. Build a reusable pipeline.

Step 3

Test and Iterate

Launch on itch.io first (instant). Gather feedback. Iterate. Then submit to Unity Asset Store for revenue.
