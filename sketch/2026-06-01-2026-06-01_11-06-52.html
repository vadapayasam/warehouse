What Changed This Week

AI-Augmented Workflows Are The Default

82% of design leaders say their org's need for designers increased or stayed the same per Figma's 2026 survey. AI isn't replacing designers — it's removing the friction between "uncertainty" and "execution." The winning workflow stack now spans ChatGPT (thinking) → Uizard (ideation) → Moonchild AI (UI generation) → Figma (polish) → Attention Insight (validation) → Claude (documentation).

"AI Experience Designer" Is A Real Job

Not a buzzword. Companies are hiring for it. Conversational interfaces, generative tools, and adaptive systems introduce interaction patterns traditional UX never addressed. Someone has to design how users interact with AI — prompts, feedback loops, output explanations. This is a new specialization, not a renamed generalist role.

The Terminal Is Becoming A Design Surface

Claude Design (April 2026) lets PMs and marketers generate interactive prototypes from natural language — no Figma, no designer. Figma fired back with Figma Make + Sites + Buzz + Draw at Config 2025. Codex (82.7% Terminal-Bench), Claude Code (Opus 4.7, 1M context), Cursor ($2B ARR). Designers don't need to write code, but they need to understand what's feasible via agents and where human judgment is still required. The boundary between design and engineering is the blurriest it's ever been.
