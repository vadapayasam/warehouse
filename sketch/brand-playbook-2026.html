Personal Branding 2026

Build a brand
that follows the code.

A 5-step framework, applied to your context. You already do the work. This is about making sure people see it.

1. Start with the end in mind

What outcome do you want? What do you need to be known for to get there? What do you have to do to earn that reputation? What do you need to learn first? That sequence is your roadmap.

Your endgame

Be the person who defined AI-native IT operations. When someone thinks "open-source MSP tools" or "AI agents for IT," your name should surface in the first three results. Not from SEO spam, but from real work people have seen you ship.

2. Find your contrarian belief

Brands don't pop from tactics. They pop from a different worldview, repeated over and over. The two-column exercise: left side, everything your space does that you find cringe or wrong. Right side, the exact opposite.

YOUR TWO COLUMNS

Status quo (cringe or wrong)

  • MSP software looks like Windows 95. Ugly, dense, overwhelming.
  • Locked into per-device pricing. You pay forever.
  • RMM tools take weeks to deploy and configure.
  • IT ops is reactive firefighting. Alerts, dashboards, panic.
  • Enterprise MSP products are closed-source and slow to change.
  • "AI in IT" means adding a chatbot that can't actually do anything.

Your opposite

  • MSP tools should be beautiful. Design-first, because ops deserves it.
  • Open-source, transparent pricing. Own your infrastructure.
  • AI-native. The LLM loop is the orchestrator, not a bolt-on.
  • Proactive calm. AI detects and fixes before the user notices.
  • Fast iteration. Ship weekly. Break things publicly. Improve.
  • AI that actually executes actions, not just recommends them.
Your contrarian thesis (repeat this everywhere)

"MSP tools don't need to be bloated enterprise garbage. AI-native, open-source, designed-first. The incumbents have terrible UX and are overpriced. AI makes it possible to build better with less."

Where to plant this flag

  • X/Twitter bio: "Building the open-source future of MSP IT management. AI-native RMM that actually works."
  • GitHub profile README: Lead with MSPclaw, your contrarian take, and your open-source philosophy.
  • LinkedIn headline: "Lead PD @ SuperOps. Building MSPclaw — AI-native open-source RMM. Design + AI for IT ops."
  • Every post: Contrast your approach with the status quo. Don't name names, just describe the better way.

5. Make case studies your content

Share wins you got for clients or yourself. This shifts perception from "you are successful" to "you create success for others." That's when trust goes through the roof.

Case studies you can write this week

  1. How I reduced my server management workload by 80% using AI agents. Show MSPclaw in action. Before/after. Real metrics.
  2. What I learned designing a modern RMM interface. Walk through MSPclaw's UI decisions. Contrast with Datto, NinjaOne, ConnectWise.
  3. AI-native vs bolt-on AI: why your MSP tool's "AI feature" is lying to you. Technical comparison. Show the architecture gap.
  4. How Agent Tina handles my vault curation autonomously. Meta case study about your own system working for you.
  5. From zero to production RMM in 6 months. The honest journey of building MSPclaw.
Format for each case study

Problem → What I tried → What actually worked → Results (real numbers) → What I'd do differently. No filler. No "elevate your workflow." Just real decisions and outcomes.

The 90 Day Plan

One thing per week. No more. Consistency beats intensity.

Month 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

Week 1: Fix GitHub profile

Add profile README with contrarian thesis, pinned MSPclaw + Agent Tina + networth, write narrative bio. Remove repos that don't represent you (javascript-basics).

Week 2: Create X account (or revitalize existing one)

Bio with contrarian take. First posts: MSPclaw intro thread, why you're building it, what makes it different.

Week 3: Publish first "Building MSPclaw" post

Deep architecture walkthrough. Why AI-native RMM exists. Publish to X, LinkedIn, and warehouse. Repurpose the same content across platforms.

Week 4: Two-column exercise content

Write your contrarian take as a standalone post. "5 things wrong with MSP tools (and how AI fixes them)." This is your signature piece.

Month 2: Distribution (Weeks 5-8)

Week 5: First MSPclaw screencast

Record a 3-minute terminal session. Show MSPclaw resolving an issue in real time. Post to X.

Week 6: Case study #1 published

Pick one from the list above. Full writeup on warehouse, threaded version on X.

Week 7: Engage with MSP community

Comment usefully on 5 posts per week in MSP subreddits, X threads, LinkedIn. No self-promotion. Just helpful.

Week 8: Cross-link everything

Warehouse gets a proper index page. Each sketch references its X thread. GitHub README links to warehouse. Bio links to everything.

Month 3: Amplify (Weeks 9-12)

Week 9: Write for a publication

Submit a technical post to dev.to, Hacker News, or an MSP industry blog. "Building an AI-native RMM with MLX and FastAPI."

Week 10: Case study #2 + metrics

Second case study. Include a "what I measured" section with real numbers.

Week 11: Open-source contribution outside MSPclaw

Contribute to MLX, LangChain, or another project MSPclaw depends on. Shows you're part of the ecosystem, not just building in isolation.

Week 12: Audit and adjust

Review: what got engagement? What felt natural? Double down on what worked. Cut what didn't.

Ignore these

Impressions, reach, profile views, "engagement rate" without context, virality on a single post. One good post going mini-viral is a distraction. Ten consistently useful posts are a brand.

You already do the real work. You ship open-source. You publish research. You build things that work. This playbook isn't about becoming someone else. It's about making sure people see what you're already doing.

Do epic shit worth following. If you haven't done dope things yet, be the vessel. Share the journey, the trial and error. Let people learn from your mistakes. That's your edge on the way up.
