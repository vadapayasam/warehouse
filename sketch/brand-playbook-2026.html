Brand Playbook 2026

A practical 4-week roadmap for a designer who builds AI-native iOS products.

Based on the 5-step brand foundation framework · Tailored for Prabha

The Diagnosis

You build across three lanes - iOS apps, AI-native agents, and product design - but the world sees disconnected outputs, not the designer-engineer behind them. Your portfolio is scattered: an MSP automation tool here, an indie iOS app there, a local AI agent over here. There's no thread.

The Reddit framework says: you skipped the foundation. The fix isn't more projects. It's a worldview that ties them together.

Where you are (the cringe column)
  • Projects announced, then forgotten
  • iOS dev? AI builder? MSP founder? No one knows
  • Building in silence - no journey shared
  • Repos look like separate people built them
  • Design taste invisible in public work
Where you're going (the opposite column)
  • One narrative: designer who builds AI-native products
  • Consistent voice - sharp, visual, unfiltered
  • Share the process, not just the ship
  • Projects linked by aesthetic + philosophy
  • Design is visible in everything you publish

The Contrarian Belief

"Most AI tools are built by engineers who don't understand design. And most design tools are built by designers who don't understand AI."

Your contrarian frame: Design is the product. AI is the material. Code is the medium. Every project - iOS apps, Agent Tina, MSPclaw - shares one belief: tools should feel as good as they work, and the people building them should understand both halves.

Your brand statement

"I design and build AI-native products. iOS apps, autonomous agents, and tools where design and engineering are the same act."

The 5-Step Framework

Step 1

Reverse-engineer your brand journey

Outcome: Known as the designer who builds AI-native iOS products. To be known for that: ship 3 iOS apps publicly, document AI agent architecture, publish design critiques. To do that: finish current iOS app, write Agent Tina case study, start a design-in-code newsletter.

Step 2

Your contrarian belief = content engine

"AI tools built without design taste are ugly and unusable. Design tools built without AI understanding are obsolete." This one belief generates 20+ tweet ideas, 5 blog topics, and the framing for every project announcement. Repeat it until people hear your name and think: that designer who actually ships.

Step 3

Optimize for trust, not virality

Stop tracking likes and retweets. Track: DMs from designers who tried your approach, GitHub Issues with thoughtful feedback, people building on your open-source tools, App Store reviews that mention the design. These are trust signals. Virality is noise.

Step 4

Intentional pairing

You're already doing this - design + code, iOS + AI, product + infrastructure. Double down. Be the person who can critique a Figma file and then write the Swift patch to fix it. Speak at design conferences about AI. Speak at AI meetups about design. Own the gap.

Step 5

Case studies as content

Case study 1: "Designing an AI agent with personality - the Tina playbook." Case study 2: "I shipped an iOS app solo in 3 weeks - here's every design decision." Case study 3: "From Figma to production: how I design UI for AI agents." Each establishes: this person ships beautiful, functional things.

4-Week Sprint

WEEK 1 - Foundation

Define your frame + clean up profiles

Write your contrarian belief. Do the two-column exercise properly. Update X bio, LinkedIn headline, GitHub profile README. Plant the flag: one thread introducing "designer who builds AI-native iOS products."

WEEK 2 - First case study

Ship the Agent Tina story

Write and publish your first case study: what Tina is, why you designed her this way, what broke, what worked. Publish on X (thread) and LinkedIn (article). Cross-post to r/design and AI communities.

WEEK 3 - Consistency loop

3 posts/day, 1 thread, 1 LinkedIn

Daily posting rhythm established. One design take. One build-in-public update. One interaction (reply to someone in your space). Weekend: write weeknotes with screenshots.

WEEK 4 - Amplify

Collaborate + expand reach

Reply to design Twitter accounts. Quote-tweet takes with your perspective. Offer to review someone's app UI publicly. Submit a talk to a design conference. End week: audit - what got trust signals (DMs, GitHub stars, collaboration requests) vs vanity metrics.
