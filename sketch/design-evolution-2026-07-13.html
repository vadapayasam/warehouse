Figma Make Gets GPT-5.6

Jul 9, 2026

Figma dropped GPT-5.6 into Figma Make, its AI prototyping engine. From our early tests, GPT-5.6 raises both the quality and speed of what Make produces on a first pass. Clean UI, responsive layouts, high-fidelity interactions - all from a prompt.

Self-healing builds. When a build hits an error, GPT-5.6 investigates and fixes itself instead of stopping. Less back-and-forth to reach a working prototype.

Stock tracking app eval. One prompt produced an interactive dashboard with sample prices, performance figures, keyboard shortcuts, and search - all in a single pass with higher token efficiency.

Available on all plans. Just open the model picker in Figma Make and choose GPT-5.6. No extra integration needed.

The Figma blog ran a demo with a stock-picking app in a dark gothic aesthetic - dense data layouts, amber-on-black sparklines, modular grids. Single prompt, working prototype, no iteration. That's the bar GPT-5.6 sets for 0-to-1 prototyping.

VS Code 1.128 Ships with Agent-First Features

Jul 8, 2026

VS Code 1.128 landed with a focus on agentic workflows. The headline: Copilot Vision goes GA - attach images and PDFs to chat by pasting, dragging, or dropping. No more describing UI bugs in words when you can just drop a screenshot.

Multi-chat Claude sessions. Run several related chats in one Claude agent session. Compare approaches, branch from earlier turns, run work in parallel - each chat keeps its own history, title, and model selection.

Agentic browser tools GA. Agents can now navigate pages, inspect content, capture screenshots, and validate web apps directly in VS Code. Enabled by default.

OS-level keyboard shortcuts. Trigger VS Code commands with keybindings that work even when VS Code isn't focused. Massively underrated productivity upgrade.

Cost visibility. See total session credit usage, subagent breakdowns, and dashboard-level tracking. Enterprise teams can finally audit what their agents are spending.

The GitHub Copilot June recap (published alongside) adds model provider discovery from the Marketplace - find extensions that contribute models without leaving the editor. Kimi K2.7 also landed for Copilot Business and Enterprise subscribers on July 7.

GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, Luna: Three-Tier Pricing War

Jul 9, 2026

OpenAI released GPT-5.6 as a product line - not a single model. Three tiers named after celestial bodies, priced to undercut Anthropic at every level. The pricing is the announcement.

Sol ($5/$30 per M tokens) - flagship. Scores within 1 point of Claude Fable 5 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, in 61% less time, at roughly half the cost.

Terra ($2.50/$15) - mid-tier, undercuts everything Anthropic sells.

Luna ($1/$6) - budget tier for high-volume, low-stakes tasks.

Features borrowed from Anthropic. Effort dial that goes past "xhigh" to "max", an "ultra" mode that fans out four parallel subagents (ultracode rebranded), explicit prompt-cache breakpoints.

The catch. SWE-bench Pro: Fable 5 scores 80.0, Sol scores 64.6 - landed within rounding error of Grok 4.5 (64.7). The crown didn't change heads. It got repriced.

Free users get Terra. Sol, max, and ultra ladder up through paid plans. Every latency and cost figure is OpenAI's own estimate of production behavior ("simulated offline"), not a measurement. The fine print matters.

Anthropic Counterpunches: Fable 5 Extended, Limits Reset

Jul 10 - 12, 2026

At 10:30 AM Pacific on Thursday, OpenAI posted GPT-5.6 rollout. At 11:01 AM - thirty-one minutes later - Anthropic's developer account posted exactly one sentence: "We've reset 5-hour and weekly rate limits for all users." No blog post. No changelog. Five million views in half a day.

Three days later (July 12), Anthropic doubled down: Fable 5 promotional access extended through July 19, 11:59 PM PT, with 50% higher Claude Code weekly limits. The extension came after the original July 7 cutoff and the subsequent July 10 rate-limit reset.

Fable 5 stays. Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise users can use Fable 5 for up to 50% of their weekly usage limit through July 19.

Rate limits reset globally. Every user got their 5-hour and weekly limits restored - a direct response to the GPT-5.6 migration wave.

Polymarket was wrong. Prediction markets priced 25% chance of extension. Anthropic extended anyway - the access whiplash problem continues.

The timing is everything. OpenAI launches GPT-5.6 at half Fable 5's price. Anthropic responds in 31 minutes with a rate-limit reset, then extends Fable 5 access a second time. This is the AI model economy in real-time - every product decision in this space is now measured against a competitor's timeline.

Signal vs Noise

This week was dominated by the GPT-5.6 / Fable 5 price war - it affects every tool chain that uses foundation models underneath. For designers and product builders, the Figma Make upgrade is the most directly relevant: GPT-5.6 raises the floor on what a single-prompt prototype looks like.

VS Code 1.128's agent-first features (multi-chat Claude sessions, Copilot Vision GA, agentic browser tools) signal where Microsoft is betting - the editor becomes an agent orchestration layer, not just a text buffer.

The signal to watch next week: does Anthropic extend Fable 5 again past July 19, or does it finally go pay-per-token? Either way, the pricing dynamics shift permanently.
