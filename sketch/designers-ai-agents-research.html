Designers + AI Agents

What professional designers are actually doing with AI agents today, backed by research and real product launches.

June 2026  ·  Sources: Figma Blog, arXiv 2509.20731

Two big things happened in 2025-2026. Figma shipped a native AI agent into the canvas. And researchers ran a design fiction study with 10 professional designers to imagine what agentic AI should actually do for them. The results line up.

Here's the breakdown of what's proven, what designers want, and what comes next.

The 5 AI Agent Roles

From Imagining Design Workflows in Agentic AI Futures (arXiv 2509.20731, 2025). A study with 10 professional designers identified five distinct roles designers want AI agents to play:

📋

Work Coordinator

Plans, summarises meetings, creates task lists, keeps the team focused on priorities.

🗂️

Resource Steward

Automates organising, renaming, tagging, and surfacing assets. Can purposefully misplace things to keep you alert.

🛡️

Guardian

Maintains context awareness, tracks browsing history, auto-saves, version control, stays within permissions.

🔄

Reframer

Suggests counter examples, alternative viewpoints, reminds of missed ideas, nudges exploration.

💡

Creative Catalyst

Generates novel ideas as jumping-off points, identifies patterns across team ideas, offers dynamic fidelity modes.

Proven Use Cases

Real products and real workflows. These are not hypotheticals: they exist today.

  • ✓ Auto-generate 3+ style variations from a single design: organic, modern, retro. Figma Agent does this natively.
  • ✓ Bulk component operations: rename variables, swap components across screens, apply design system updates to 200+ frames at once.
  • ✓ Design system documentation: automatically generate docs for components, states, variants with contextual examples.
  • ✓ Feedback summarization: sort long comment threads by theme, turn critique into an actionable revision plan.
  • ✓ Code-to-canvas handoffs: via MCP servers. Drop layers from Claude/GPT into Figma, iterate with the agent, send back.
  • ✓ Interactive prototyping from prompts: Figma Make turns a product brief into a testable prototype in minutes.

What Designers Want

From the research study: direct quotes and patterns from 10 professional designers:

🎯 Knows my system

AI that understands your components, tokens, and patterns. No generic output.

✋ Suggest, don't decide

Non-destructive suggestions. The final call stays with the designer.

🤝 Collaborator, not replacement

AI as a thinking partner: reframing ideas, not replacing the creative process.

🔍 Low-fi first

"Lower fidelity allows people to feel more comfortable to critique" - P07 Julia

⏱️ Traceable history

Version control you can navigate. "There's no way of tracing back to time points" - P05 Elton

🚪 Client vs internal separation

A separate space for clients to comment without changing the design itself.

The Pattern Is Clear

AI agents are not replacing designers. They're taking over the work designers never wanted to do: renaming layers, generating variations, documenting components, maintaining consistency. The creative decisions stay with the human.

The designers who win with this technology will be the ones who use agents as amplifiers, not crutches. The agent explores. The designer decides.

"Rather than acting as a tool and a completely neutral party, it could come in with counter examples: offering alternative viewpoints on how you are going about the process."
- P06 Frank, study participant
