New Finds

Kaseya Platform GA · April 28, 2026

Kaseya Intelligence + Digital Specialists: "First Agentic IT Management Platform"

Three-layer architecture: Kaseya Intelligence (data/AI engine, 1B+ tickets, 3 exabytes backup, 17M endpoints), Execution layer (autonomous action), Digital Specialists (agentic AI workers).

Digital Specialist for Ticket Triage uses a Hybrid Reasoning Architecture - a 5-agent mesh:

  • Impact Agent (LLM) - priority, issue type, sub-type, severity
  • Skills Assessor (LLM) - maps problems to expertise and certifications
  • Smart Assigner (deterministic) - ranks technicians by skill match, certs, past performance
  • Capacity Planner (deterministic) - real-time availability, schedules, time zones, workload
  • Critique Agent (LLM) - challenges decisions to catch bias and blind spots

Lifecycle: Learn > Triage > Review. Each ticket feeds back. Technicians accept, reject, or refine.

Early access (25+ partners): 80% fewer categorization errors; 90% decrease in mean time to assign within 2 weeks; 98% accuracy in issue/sub-issue categorization within 1 week.

Also launched: Kaseya SIEM (60+ sources, 24/7 SOC); Unified Cyber Resilience Portal (AI-driven screenshot verification >99.9%); Datto RMM Smart Insights with conversational AI.

ConnectWise Platform Early Partners · April 27, 2026

zofiQ Agentic AI Expansion: From Insights to Execution

Shift from "assistive AI to autonomous execution with oversight." Three components: AI Assistants (embedded PSA recommendations), Autonomous Agents (execute repetitive workflows), Oversight Agent (monitors tickets, SLAs, flags risks).

Partner results: 86% reduction in escalations (Fusion Computing). "Like adding two extra analysts without hiring." Auto triage saved 30+ hours (Intech Hawaii).

Key differentiator: Operates natively within ConnectWise (PSA, RMM, cybersecurity, data protection). Closed-loop workflows from intake to resolution.

ITSM.tools Research Omdia · Feb 2026

The MSP Agentic AI Execution Gap

70% of MSPs report using agentic AI internally. Only 10% deployed in IT service desk or security/compliance. 30% report no use at all. Governance is the #1 barrier (47%), not tech (16%).

Maturity model: Stage 1 Assistive (classifies, recommends) to Stage 2 Semi-autonomous (routine with approval) to Stage 3 Autonomous (cross-domain, minimal supervision). Most orgs sit in Stage 1.

PE angle: AI maturity factors into MSP valuations. Delay = exit risk.

Gap

Pricing Still Bundled

When do PSA/RMM vendors shift from "AI as differentiator" to "AI as revenue line"? Atlassian's $1/resolution is the only consumption model. The rest bundle. For MSPs, great (no meter anxiety). For vendors, AI ROI is retention/expansion, not direct revenue.

04

Strategic Implications for SuperOps

Execution Gap = Opportunity

  • 60% of MSPs stuck at pilot. The problem is not awareness. It is governance, trust, and getting from Stage 1 to Stage 2.
  • Bundled pricing is table stakes. Vendors who charge for AI directly will be outliers. Free AI in PSA/RMM is the new competitive baseline.
  • Oversight Agents are becoming required. ConnectWise zofiQ and Kaseya Critique Agent both bake governance in. This is a signal.
  • Marketplace model differentiates SuperOps. AWS-backed community agents versus closed ecosystems. Bet on openness.

Opportunity: Position SuperOps as the Stage 2 enabler - bridging the gap between assistive (what MSPs have now) and semi-autonomous (what they need next).
