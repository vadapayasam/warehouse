MSP Gap Analysis

Dev-first, not service-desk-first

New Relic has the strongest no-code agent builder and the best free tier in observability. But the product was designed for SREs at product companies, not MSPs serving 50 clients.

5-Seat Cap on Standard

Standard tier maxes at 5 full-platform users. An MSP with 5 clients x 2 engineers = 10 people. Must jump to Pro at $349/user ($3,490/mo) or Enterprise (custom). No self-serve MSP pricing tier exists.

No PSA Integration

Connects to ServiceNow and Jira for ticket creation. No ConnectWise, Autotask, or HaloPSA. No client billing workflows. No multi-tenant client separation in the standard model.

No RMM Bridge

Monitors infrastructure but doesn't manage it. No NinjaOne, ConnectWise Automate, or Datto RMM integration. Telemetry in is fine — action out is manual.

MCP = Dev-First

MCP server targets coding agents (Claude Code, Copilot, ChatGPT). No ITSM-specific MCP tools (create ticket, escalate, notify client). AI agents can query telemetry but can't triage a service desk queue.

FedRAMP + HIPAA exclusion: New Relic AI is not available on FedRAMP Moderate or HIPAA-eligible accounts outside Enterprise tier. This is a meaningful gap for federal/healthcare MSPs — they'd need to go Enterprise or use Datadog/Dynatrace which support these compliance frameworks at lower tiers. [9]
