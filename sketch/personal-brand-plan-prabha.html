Personal Brand Plan

A practical 4-week roadmap to go from builder to known builder - without the bullshit.

Based on the 5-step brand foundation framework • Tailored for Prabha

The Diagnosis

You're building great things - MSPclaw, Agent Tina, idli, iOS apps - but the brand is fragmented across platforms with no cohesive narrative. People see individual projects, not the builder behind them.

The Reddit framework says: you skipped the foundation and went straight to building. The fix isn't more content. It's a worldview that connects everything you build.

Where you are (the cringe column)
  • Projects announced, then forgotten
  • No consistent voice across platforms
  • Building in silence - no journey shared
  • Disconnected repos (MSPclaw, idli, tools)
  • "Just ship" with no follow-up narrative
Where you're going (the opposite column)
  • One narrative: "the MSP builder who codes"
  • Consistent voice - technical, sharp, honest
  • Share the journey, not just the output
  • Projects linked by your worldview
  • Ship + tell the story of how and why

The Contrarian Belief

"Most MSP tools are bloated enterprise garbage built by people who've never touched a ticket."

Your contrarian frame: Build for the operator, not the buyer. Every project - MSPclaw, Agent Tina, idli - shares one belief: MSP software should be built by people who actually run MSPs, not by sales-engineered product teams.

This is your throughline. Every tweet, every repo, every case study reinforces this worldview.

Your brand statement

"I build tools for MSP operators who are tired of enterprise bloat. Open source, agent-native, and opinionated."

The 5-Step Framework

Step 1

Reverse-engineer your brand journey

Outcome: Trusted as the go-to builder for MSP automation tools. Known for: open-source, agent-native, operator-first. To be known for that: ship MSPclaw v2, publish architecture breakdowns, speak at MSP events. To do that: finish Phase 4-5 of idli, get MSPclaw to beta users, write 3 case studies.

Step 2

Your contrarian belief = content engine

"Enterprise MSP tools are overengineered for procurement, not for the tech who uses them daily." This one belief generates 20+ tweet ideas, 5 blog post topics, and the framing for every case study. Repeat it until people associate it with you.

Step 3

Optimize for trust, not virality

Stop tracking likes and retweets. Track: DMs from people who tried your tools, GitHub Issues that aren't bug reports, "we used your approach at our MSP" replies. These are trust signals. Virality is noise.

Step 4

Intentional pairing

You're already doing this - MSPclaw + SuperOps = built by someone who knows the space. Double down. Be the person who bridges open-source communities and MSP practitioners. Write about SuperOps challenges in public. Attend MSP gatherings as "the guy who built X."

Step 5

Case studies as content

Your first case study: "How I automated 40% of my daily ops with an agent that runs on my Mac." Second: "I replaced our RMM's alerting with a local ML model - here's what broke." Third: "Migrating a monorepo from TypeScript to Swift for a 200ms menu bar app." Each establishes: this person ships, and his work has impact.

4-Week Sprint

WEEK 1 - Foundation

Define your frame + clean up profiles

Write your contrarian belief. Do the two-column exercise properly. Update X bio, LinkedIn headline, GitHub profile README. Plant the flag: one thread introducing "the MSP builder who codes."

WEEK 2 - First case study

Ship the MSPclaw story

Write and publish your first case study: what MSPclaw is, why you built it, what broke, what worked. Publish on X (thread) and LinkedIn (article). Cross-post to r/msp and relevant communities.

WEEK 3 - Consistency loop

3 posts/day, 1 thread, 1 LinkedIn

Daily posting rhythm established. One technical hot take. One build-in-public update. One interaction (reply to someone in your space). Weekend: write the weeknotes post.

WEEK 4 - Amplify

Collaborate + expand reach

Reply to MSP Twitter accounts. Quote-tweet their takes with your perspective. Offer to build something small for someone and document it. Submit a talk to an MSP conference. End week: audit - what got trust signals (DMs, GitHub stars, replies) vs vanity metrics.
