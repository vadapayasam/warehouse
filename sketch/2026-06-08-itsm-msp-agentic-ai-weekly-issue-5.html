New Finds

Kaseya: "First Agentic IT Management Platform" — Kaseya Intelligence + Digital Specialists

Kaseya unveiled what it calls the first agentic IT management platform at Kaseya Connect Global (April 28, 2026). The architecture has three layers:

1. Kaseya Intelligence — the data/AI engine, trained on 1 billion+ help desk tickets, 3 exabytes of backup data, 17 million managed endpoints.

2. Execution layer — autonomously takes action across IT operations, cybersecurity, and cyber resilience.

3. Digital Specialists — agentic AI workers that handle high-volume tasks.

Digital Specialist for Ticket Triage (GA now for Autotask Ultimate) uses a Hybrid Reasoning Architecture — a coordinated mesh of 5 specialized agents:
  • Impact Agent (LLM-powered) — evaluates priority, issue type, sub-type, severity.
  • Skills Assessor (LLM-powered) — maps problems to required expertise and certifications.
  • Smart Assigner (deterministic) — ranks technicians by skill match, certifications, past performance.
  • Capacity Planner (deterministic) — checks real-time availability, schedules, time zones, workload.
  • Critique Agent (LLM-powered) — challenges decisions made by other agents to catch bias/blind spots.
Lifecycle: Learn > Triage > Review. Each resolved ticket feeds back into Kaseya Intelligence. Technicians can accept, reject, or refine recommendations. The system captures what was done, identifies skills used, and improves the reasoning mesh. Early access results (25+ partners):
  • 80% reduction in categorization errors (Advisor ICT)
  • 90% decrease in mean time to assign (Kaseya internal) within 2 weeks
  • 98% accuracy in issue/sub-issue categorization (Vitis Technologies) within 1 week
Other launches in the same announcement:
  • Kaseya SIEM (GA) — 60+ data sources, cross-surface correlation, automated response, 24/7 SOC option.
  • Unified Cyber Resilience Portal — on-prem + SaaS + endpoint + cloud backup in one portal, AI-driven screenshot verification >99.9% accuracy.
  • Datto RMM — Smart Insights (AI activity log, AI device insights) with conversational AI.

ConnectWise: zofiQ Agentic AI Expansion — From Insights to Execution

ConnectWise announced zofiQ expansion (April 27, 2026), positioning it as the shift from "assistive AI to autonomous execution with oversight."

zofiQ components:
  • AI Assistants — embedded in PSA for recommendations.
  • Autonomous Agents — execute repetitive workflows (ticket triage, routing, admin tasks).
  • Oversight Agent — governance layer; monitors tickets, SLAs, agent activity; proactively flags risks and inconsistencies.
Partner results:
  • 86% reduction in escalations (Fusion Computing)
  • "Like adding two extra analysts without hiring" (Fusion Computing)
  • Auto triage saved 30+ hours of Microsoft configuration work (Intech Hawaii)
  • Company assignment agent alone saved 30+ hours (Intech Hawaii)
Key differentiator: zofiQ operates natively within the ConnectWise platform (PSA, RMM, cybersecurity, data protection), not as a standalone tool. Closed-loop workflows from intake through resolution.

SuperOps: Agentic AI Marketplace (AWS Partnership) + MSP Guide

SuperOps launched the Agentic AI Marketplace (beta September 2025, AWS-backed), positioned as a trusted destination where MSPs select ready-to-use agents and developers publish/monetize innovations.

Ecosystem:
  • Agentic AI Community — hub for MSPs, IT leaders, developers to collaborate.
  • SuperHack Hackathon — global developer challenge, $100,000 prize pool, winning submissions featured in Marketplace.
SuperOps MSP guide to agentic AI (published early 2026) outlines six core agentic AI use cases for MSPs:

1. Autonomous remediation (detect > analyze > fix automatically)

2. Smart ticket handling (classify, prioritize, assign, resolve L1)

3. Automated compliance and patching

4. Platform integration (RMM + PSA + alerting + documentation)

5. Proactive asset management

6. Predictive maintenance and forecasting

Market context: Canalys data cited: 90% of MSPs exploring AI, 61% stuck at pilot stage, 77% feel pressure to deliver AI-powered tools to clients.

NinjaOne: Patch Intelligence AI

NinjaOne launched Patch Intelligence AI for Windows patch management. Features:

  • KB Sentiment Analysis — analyzes patches for stability, known bugs, aggregated user sentiment.
  • Multi-Status Patch Evaluation — auto-categorizes: "Appears Stable", "Caution", "Known Issues".
  • Flexible AI Integration — admins can enable/disable per tenant.
Positioning: Tactical AI augmentation (patching decisions), not full agentic autonomy. Competes with Kaseya's >99.9% AI screenshot verification and Datto's patch workflows.

ITSM.tools: The MSP Agentic AI Execution Gap

Omdia research (commissioned by SuperOps, via Candefero/Canalys databases) published February 2026:

  • 70% of MSPs report using agentic AI internally.
  • Only 10% have primarily deployed it in IT service desk or security/compliance.
  • 30% report no use of agentic AI at all.
  • Governance is the #1 barrier (47%), not technical expertise (16%).
  • Enterprise cost savings: $250–1,200 per employee annually for agentic AI employee support.
  • MSP labor cost: ~40% of costs are labor. Level 1 ticket resolution = obvious starting point.
Maturity model (Omdia):

1. Assistive — classifies, recommends, speeds human-led workflows.

2. Semi-autonomous — executes routine tasks with human approval.

3. Autonomous — coordinates across IT, security, cloud with minimal supervision.

Most orgs sit in stage 1, some beginning stage 2. Genuine cross-domain autonomy remains aspirational. Private equity angle: AI operational maturity is beginning to factor into MSP valuations. Delaying adoption = strategic and financial risk for MSPs considering exit.

Gap: Pricing Still Bundled

The big open question: when do PSA/RMM vendors shift from "AI as platform differentiator" to "AI as revenue line item"? Atlassian's $1/resolution model is the only consumption pricing in the space. Kaseya, ConnectWise, and SuperOps are all bundling. For MSPs, this is great (no meter anxiety). For vendors, it means AI ROI is measured in retention and expansion, not direct AI revenue.
