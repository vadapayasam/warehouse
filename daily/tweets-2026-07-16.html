3 interesting 12:00 PM
Your Mac Mini can run a 27-billion-parameter model at 9 tokens per second. For free. Forever. I spent yesterday wiring a 1-bit quantized model called Bonsai into my vault synthesis pipeline. It loads in 4 seconds. It generates 4096 tokens in 440 seconds. It thinks out loud in XML tags. It is weird and slow and completely private. A year ago this would have required a $500 per month API budget and a data pipeline to OpenAI. Now it runs while I make coffee. The output is not always better than GPT-5.5. Sometimes it is worse. But it is mine. No rate limits. No usage caps. No wondering if my financial data is training someone else's model. The real constraint is not speed. It is patience. 9 tokens per second feels slow until you realize you can run it 100 times a day for zero dollars. Cloud APIs are fast because they charge by the millisecond. Local models are slow because they charge nothing. I will take slow and free over fast and rented every time.

4 workflow 4:00 PM
I deleted five files from my vault yesterday and it felt like surgery. Dead weight. A word-count template filler. A pipeline self-audit that catalogued its own errors. An overnight analysis synthesis that was just a list of things that broke. None of them taught me anything I could use. I replaced them with one rule: daily synthesis follows three pillars only. Patterns learned. Architecture decisions. Domain knowledge. If a file does not fit one of those, it does not get written. This sounds obvious. It took me three months to enforce it. The temptation to log everything is strong. Every error feels important when you are debugging it. Most errors are not important three days later. The ones that are become patterns. The ones that are not become noise. The three-pillar rule is not about minimalism. It is about respecting my own attention. I will forget most of what I read. I should at least forget the right things.
