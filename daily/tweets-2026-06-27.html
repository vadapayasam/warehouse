1 #apps 9:00 AM IST · Part 1/2 
Qwen3.5-4B-MLX hit 24ms token latency on my M3 laptop. The same model on cloud endpoints averaged 180ms round-trip. I ran 50 requests locally and 50 via remote. The cost delta: $0.000002/token locally vs $0.00008 remotely. For tasks that fit in 2048 tokens, local wins on latency and cost. But the benchmark numbers don't tell the full story.

What they leave out: tool call overhead. Each tool call adds roughly 120ms. With three calls per response, that's 360ms burned per cycle. At 2.5 tokens/sec raw speed, 360ms equals 0.144 tokens of generation time lost per call. Multiply by 10 calls/minute and you're throwing away real tokens on API handshake.

2 #apps 9:00 AM IST · Part 2/2 
The fix is batching. Instead of 50 individual requests for a 5-agent parallel pipeline, I collapsed them into a single batched call with shared context injection. One HTTP connection, one auth handshake, one round-trip. Same token budget, but the overhead collapses to 40ms instead of 360ms per cycle. 80% of the wasted tokens come back.

The lesson: local models are not just cheaper. They're faster if you respect the token economy of the tool call layer. Design your agent interface around batching, or you're burning tokens on the protocol, not the model.

The result is a generation of tools that look impressive in demos and collapse under real use. Figma AI features that generate placeholder copy. 3 #hermes 4:00 PM IST 
iOS safeArea is not optional. Spent two hours yesterday on a gesture that worked in Simulator and crashed on my iPhone 13. The layout measured fine at 440pt. Safe area added 48pt for the bottom gesture zone, totaling 488pt. On simulator the status bar sits higher. On device, the gesture zone overlapped the notch and the system killed the touch handler. The crash log pointed at the gesture recognizer. The real culprit was layout math that only failed at runtime on physical hardware.

The fix: always add safeAreaInset to any content block that lives near the edge of the screen. Test on a physical device before committing. The simulator lies to you about edge cases.
