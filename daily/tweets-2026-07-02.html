The team could have shipped v0.18 with new features. They spent ten days closing 949 issues instead. That is the judgment call most teams get wrong: new features feel like progress. Fixing old problems feels like housekeeping. But an agent that breaks in known ways is an agent users stop trusting. Trust is a function of reliability, not capability. The release name is not marketing. It is a design decision about what the team actually shipped.

4 apps 4:00 PM
Apple updated App Store rejection policy for 2026. Three changes that actually matter: 1. Account deletion is now mandatory. Not a link to a web form. A functional button inside the app. 2. Age ratings expanded to 13+, 16+, and 18+ tiers. 3. CI rejections happen before a reviewer touches the build. Teams with outdated pipelines get blocked at upload. The third one is the real shift. Used to be a reviewer would catch it. Now the pipeline catches it. Colder, faster, and ultimately more fair because it is consistent. Automated enforcement at scale is not coming. It is here.
